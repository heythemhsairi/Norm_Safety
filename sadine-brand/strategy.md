## 0. The One Sentence

> **Sadine Tours organizes premium travel for people who refuse to choose between elegance and ease.**

Everything in this document — every color decision, every type weight, every photographic frame — exists to support that sentence.

---

## 1. Strategic Foundation

### 1.1 The Repositioning

For a long time the Tunisian travel agency category has competed on the same axis: who can quote the cheapest package the fastest. The result is a market that *all looks the same* — same WhatsApp PDFs, same green-and-white logos, same crowded photography, same stock airplanes flying over the same monuments.

Sadine Tours is repositioning out of that category entirely.

We are not the cheapest agency. We are not the loudest. We are the agency that *organizes* — and the organization itself is the luxury. We're closer to a private travel concierge than to a discount intermediary.

### 1.2 Positioning Statement

> For modern Tunisian and Maghreb travelers who want a curated, organized, premium travel experience without the friction of doing it themselves, **Sadine Tours** is a digital-first travel house that designs, books, and orchestrates the entire journey — from boarding pass to last sunset — with the precision of a luxury airline and the warmth of a private host.
> Unlike traditional agencies that sell packages, Sadine Tours sells **calm, taste, and time saved**.

### 1.3 Mission

To make organized, premium travel a default — not a privilege. To take every fragment of the journey (visa, flight, transfer, hotel, restaurant, guide, return) and present it as a single, smooth, beautifully designed experience.

### 1.4 Vision (2030)

To become the most trusted modern travel brand in the Maghreb — the first name a new generation of professionals, couples, and families thinks of when they decide to leave home well.

### 1.5 Brand Promise

Three things, every time:
1. **Nothing forgotten.** Every detail handled before you ask.
2. **Nothing ugly.** Every touchpoint, from the email to the airport pickup sign, is designed.
3. **Nothing rushed.** The pace of the brand is the pace of someone who has already taken care of everything.

## 2. Brand Values

| # | Value | What it means in practice |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Precision** | A flight time is a flight time. A confirmation is a confirmation. Nothing is "approximate." |
| 2 | **Elegance** | Surfaces, language, and gestures are quiet. Loud is a sign of effort; we don't want effort to show. |
| 3 | **Warmth** | The brand is premium but not cold. There is always a human voice underneath the system. |
| 4 | **Curation** | We do not sell every destination. We sell the ones we have personally vetted. |
| 5 | **Modernity** | We are a digital-first house. Booking, communication, and follow-up are app-native, not paper-native. |
| 6 | **Discretion** | What we know about a client (preferences, allergies, anniversaries) is held quietly, used invisibly. |

## 3. Brand Personality

If Sadine Tours were a person, they would be:

- **Late thirties.** Old enough to know how things should be done, young enough to expect it on a phone.
- **Bilingual** — French, Arabic, fluent English. Switches without translating.
- **Trained somewhere precise** — hotel school in Lausanne, an airline in Doha, a private concierge in Paris.
- **Unflashy.** Wears one good watch, not three.
- **Has time for you.** Doesn't seem busy even when they are.
- **Notices things** — the temperature of the car, the light at check-in, whether you've eaten.

This person is **not** a salesman. They are a host.

## 4. Tone of Voice

### 4.1 Voice Principles

| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| Warm | Casual |
| Confident | Loud |
| Specific | Generic |
| Brief | Brusque |
| Bilingual | Mixed-up |
| Refined | Stiff |

### 4.2 Writing Rules

1. **Short sentences.** Premium feels calm; calm sounds short.
2. **Specific nouns.** "A 7am pickup in a black sedan" beats "convenient airport transfer."
3. **No exclamation marks** outside of social captions.
4. **No emojis** in customer service. Selectively in social.
5. **Honor the client's language.** If they wrote in Arabic, reply in Arabic. If French, French. Never auto-translate.
6. **Never apologize for price.** State it once, with confidence.

### 4.3 Sample Lines

**Onboarding email — opening line**
> Welcome, Mr. Ben Aïcha. Your journey begins on Tuesday. Everything below is yours to keep — itinerary, contacts, and the small things we've already arranged.

**Booking confirmation — subject**
> Confirmed · Istanbul · 12 → 17 May

**Social caption (FR)**
> *Cinq jours à Istanbul. Tout est prêt.*

**Social caption (AR)**
> ‎خمسة أيام في إسطنبول. كل شيء جاهز.

**Customer service — handling a delay**
> Your flight is delayed by two hours. We've already moved your transfer and notified the hotel. You don't need to do anything.

## 5. Audience Archetypes

We design for three primary archetypes. They overlap; one brand serves all three.

### 5.1 *The Quiet Professional* (primary)
- 32–48 years old, Tunisian or Maghrebi, urban (Tunis, Sfax, Sousse, Casablanca, Algiers).
- Senior professional, entrepreneur, doctor, executive.
- Travels 4–8 times a year — half business, half leisure.
- Values **time** above price. Is willing to pay for invisible labor.
- Books on phone, reads in French and Arabic, expects English to work.
- Trigger phrase: *"I just don't have time to organize this."*

### 5.2 *The Modern Couple* (secondary)
- 28–40, dual income, no children or one young child.
- Travels for honeymoons, anniversaries, "we both need a break" weekends.
- Highly visual — cares how the trip *looks* on Instagram, but not in a shallow way; they want **taste**.
- Trigger phrase: *"I want it to feel like a movie."*

### 5.3 *The Considered Family* (tertiary)
- 38–55, two to three children.
- Will not compromise on safety, hygiene, or the children's experience.
- Wants the **adults' trip preserved** even with kids in tow — i.e. real hotels, real food, real culture.
- Trigger phrase: *"I don't want a kids' resort. I want a real trip the kids can also enjoy."*

The functional promise is *"we organize your travel."* That is table stakes.

## 8. Logo System (overview)

The wordmark is the dominant asset. Full anatomy is illustrated in the visual book; what follows is the *intent*.

- **Wordmark**: rounded geometric sans, custom-finished. The `n` and `e` are joined by a single elegant arc — a small but unmistakable signature.
- **The `i`**: the dot is replaced by a small bird, drawn as a continuous gesture. The bird also reads as an abstract `S` — the brand monogram hides inside the wordmark.
- **"TOURS"**: set beneath, generous tracking, light weight, framed by two thin rules — an airline-departure-board cue.
- **Bird color**: gold (#F5B942). Always. The bird is the only place gold lives in the primary mark; this is what gives it luxury weight.

### Lockups
1. **Primary horizontal** — full Sadine Tours wordmark.
2. **Stacked vertical** — for app icons, profile pictures, square applications.
3. **Icon-only (bird)** — for app icon, favicon, social profile, embroidery, lapel pin.
4. **Monochrome white** — for photography, video, dark backgrounds.
5. **Monochrome dark purple** — for light backgrounds, stationery.
6. **One-color print** — for engraving, embossing, foil stamping.

### Clear Space
A safety zone equal to the height of the bird icon (`x`) on every side. Never crop into it.

### Minimum Sizes
- Digital: 96 px wide for the full lockup; 24 px for the icon.
- Print: 25 mm wide for the full lockup; 8 mm for the icon.

### Misuses (forbidden)
- Do not stretch.
- Do not recolor the wordmark in non-brand colors.
- Do not place on busy photography without a darkening overlay.
- Do not rotate.
- Do not outline.
- Do not add drop shadows or bevels.
- Do not separate the bird from the `i` — the bird is part of the word.

## 9. Color System (overview)

| Role | Name | HEX | RGB | CMYK |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Sadine Purple | `#4B2E83` | 75 · 46 · 131 | 43 · 65 · 0 · 49 |
| Depth | Midnight Plum | `#2A124F` | 42 · 18 · 79 | 47 · 77 · 0 · 69 |
| Accent | Sadine Gold | `#F5B942` | 245 · 185 · 66 | 0 · 24 · 73 · 4 |
| Surface | Soft White | `#F8F6FC` | 248 · 246 · 252 | 2 · 2 · 0 · 1 |
| Tint | Light Lavender | `#D8C8F3` | 216 · 200 · 243 | 11 · 18 · 0 · 5 |

### Usage Ratio (recommended)
- 60% — Sadine Purple / Midnight Plum (backgrounds, hero, product chrome)
- 25% — Soft White (typography, breathing space, surfaces)
- 10% — Light Lavender (cards, secondary surfaces, soft tints)
- 5% — Sadine Gold (the bird, CTAs, single accents)

**Gold rule:** if more than one piece of gold is visible in a single composition without a deliberate reason, remove one. Gold is a punctuation mark, not a paragraph.

### Gradients
- **Primary depth** — `#2A124F → #4B2E83` (heroes, presentations).
- **Glow** — `#4B2E83 → #7B5BCE` (UI lights, social media accents, motion).
- **Luxury** — `#4B2E83 → #F5B942` (used **only** on premium moments — anniversary mailers, invite cards, never on a daily UI).

## 10. Typography System (overview)

| Role | Family | Foundry | Weights used |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display / wordmark | **Satoshi** | Indian Type Foundry / Fontshare | 700, 500, 400 |
| Text & UI | **Manrope** | Mikhail Sharanda / Google Fonts | 600, 500, 400 |
| Arabic primary | **Alexandria** | TypeTogether / Google Fonts | 700, 500, 400 |
| Arabic secondary (UI) | **Cairo** | Mohamed Gaber / Google Fonts | 500, 400 |

### Hierarchy
- **H1 — Display Editorial** · Satoshi Bold · 88 / 96 px · tracking −2%
- **H2 — Section** · Satoshi Bold · 56 / 64 px · tracking −1%
- **H3 — Subhead** · Satoshi Medium · 32 / 40 px · tracking 0
- **Eyebrow / Label** · Satoshi Medium · 12 px · tracking +12% · uppercase
- **Body Large** · Manrope Regular · 18 / 28 px
- **Body** · Manrope Regular · 15 / 24 px
- **Caption** · Manrope Medium · 12 / 16 px · tracking +4%
- **Arabic Display** · Alexandria Bold · 80 px (RTL paired with Satoshi at 88 px)
- **Arabic Body** · Alexandria Regular · 18 / 30 px

### Rules
- Generous line-height. Premium reads slow.
- Generous tracking on labels (eyebrows, button labels).
- Tight tracking on display.
- Never use italic on display. Manrope italic is allowed in body only, sparingly.
- One typeface per piece of content — do not mix Satoshi and Manrope inside a single sentence.
- Arabic and Latin paired vertically (Arabic above or below Latin), never inline mid-sentence.

## 11. Visual Language

### 11.1 Photography
- **Subject**: the *moment of arrival* — wing tip on approach, tarmac in dawn light, hotel doorway at dusk, coffee on a balcony, cabin window above clouds, hands fastening a watch, a single suitcase in a marble lobby.
- **Light**: golden hour, blue hour, low cinematic light. Avoid midday flat sun.
- **Color**: respect the brand palette in post — slight purple lift in the shadows, warm highlights, never a saturated tropical postcard look.
- **Composition**: wide negative space, single point of interest, anamorphic/cinematic aspect ratios (2.39:1) for hero, 4:5 for social.
- **People**: when shown, always elegantly dressed, never staged in a "tour group" cliché. Backs of heads, hands, and silhouettes are preferred to direct portraits.
- **Banned**: stock photos of airplanes against a flat blue sky, monument collages, neon party shots, generic "happy family" stock.

### 11.2 Iconography
- **Style**: 1.5 px stroke, rounded joins, 24 × 24 grid.
- **Family**: Phosphor (Light/Regular) as a base, with custom additions for the bird, suitcase, boarding pass, and key icons.
- **Color**: monochrome — Soft White on dark, Sadine Purple on light. Never colorful.

### 11.3 Texture
- Subtle film grain on hero photography.
- Soft glow blooms behind isolated UI elements.
- Glassmorphism (blurred translucent purple) for floating cards on dark.
- Never gradients without intent. Never hard drop shadows.

### 11.4 Motion
- Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)` — slow-out, never abrupt.
- Duration: 600–900 ms on hero transitions, 200–300 ms on UI.
- Camera language: dolly, slow pan, subtle parallax. Never zooms or shakes.
- A single signature: a soft purple halo that travels across the screen on each section change — like a runway light passing.

## 12. Social Media System

### 12.1 Feed Logic
A 3-by-3 grid, repeated. Each row of three has:
1. **Destination cinematic** (full-bleed photo, 4:5).
2. **Quote / phrase** (typographic post on Sadine Purple — Satoshi Bold, white).
3. **Detail** (close-up: a watch, a key, a coffee, a wing tip).

This makes the feed scan as: image → idea → detail. Repeat.

### 12.2 Post Templates
- **Cinematic Hero** — full image, single line of caption in lower-left, gold bird mark in upper-right.
- **Quote Card** — Sadine Purple background, white Satoshi quote (3–7 words), bird mark bottom-center.
- **Itinerary Card** — three-line itinerary on Light Lavender, Manrope, gold rule between lines.
- **Boarding Pass** — fake ticket as a teaser for a destination drop.

### 12.3 Stories
- Vertical 9:16. Always start with a 1-second purple-glow intro frame carrying the icon. Always close with the wordmark.
- One idea per story. Do not stack three.
- Captions use Satoshi Medium 28 px, white, 90% opacity, with subtle dark gradient behind for legibility.

### 12.4 Reels / TikTok
- Open on motion (pan, dolly, walking) — never on a static shot.
- First 1.2 seconds are the hook: a single line of Satoshi text revealing.
- Color grade: cinematic teal-and-orange tilted toward purple-and-gold.
- Always end on the bird icon held for 0.6 s on Sadine Purple.

### 12.5 CTA System
We use four CTAs across the brand. Use one per touchpoint.

1. **Plan with us.**
2. **Book quietly.**
3. **Leave the details to Sadine.**
4. **The trip is ready.**

## 13. Applications

### 13.1 Stationery
- Business cards: 85 × 55 mm, 600 gsm, Sadine Purple front with a **blind embossed** wordmark, gold-foil bird only. Reverse: Soft White, name in Satoshi Medium 10 pt, role in Manrope Regular 8 pt, contact stack right-aligned.
- Letterhead: Soft White, wordmark top-center, thin gold rule under wordmark, body type Manrope.
- Folder: Midnight Plum cloth-finish, gold-foil bird only on the front.

### 13.2 Travel Touchpoints
- **Boarding pass / itinerary card** (the signature artifact): 100 × 210 mm, perforated, two panels — left panel Sadine Purple with white Satoshi destination name + bird, right panel Soft White with Manrope itinerary block. Folded into a custom envelope.
- **Luggage tag**: 60 × 110 mm, Sadine Purple front (blind-embossed bird), Soft White reverse with contact stack, Sadine Gold leather strap.
- **Airport pickup signage**: 297 × 420 mm (A3), Sadine Purple, white Satoshi 96 pt name, bird upper-right. Held inside a leather portfolio.
- **Welcome envelope**: A5, Soft White, gold-foil wordmark, wax seal in Sadine Purple.

### 13.3 Environmental
- **Storefront** (Tunis flagship): floor-to-ceiling glass, interior in Midnight Plum, single backlit wordmark above the desk, gold accent line at counter height. No window posters. The window is the brand.
- **Uniform**: Midnight Plum blazer, Soft White shirt, gold lapel pin (the bird), no name tag (replaced by an embossed leather pocket band).
- **Vehicle**: matte Midnight Plum, gloss-on-matte wordmark on the rear quarter, gold bird on the front fender. No phone numbers, no URLs on the vehicle — discretion is the point.

### 13.4 Digital
- **App icon**: Sadine Purple background, gold bird center, generous padding.
- **Favicon**: bird only.
- **Website hero**: full-bleed cinematic video, Satoshi headline lower-left, single floating CTA upper-right (`Plan with us →`).
- **Booking UI**: dark mode (Midnight Plum surfaces, Soft White type), rounded 16 px corners, soft glass cards, gold only for the active step indicator.
- **Email**: text-first, Satoshi headline, Manrope body, Sadine Purple footer with the bird.

## 14. Website — Creative Direction

### 14.1 Architecture
1. **Hero** — cinematic 8-second video, Satoshi tagline, single CTA.
2. **The Promise** — three-pillar manifesto (Plan · Travel · Return).
3. **Featured Destinations** — six tiles, soft hover lift, subtle parallax.
4. **The Sadine Method** — 4-step horizontal scroll: Discover → Design → Depart → Decompress.
5. **Stories** — editorial blog cards (real client journeys, with permission).
6. **Concierge** — the booking entry point, a single conversational form.
7. **Footer** — minimal, language switch (FR / AR / EN), Instagram, the bird.

### 14.2 Animation & Transitions
- Page transitions: a single horizontal purple sweep, 700 ms.
- Section reveals: staggered word-by-word fade for headlines, 60 ms stagger.
- Hover: lift `+4px`, soft glow, no scale.
- Booking form: each step *slides* into place, never jumps.

### 14.3 Booking UX Principles
- One question per screen.
- Keyboard-friendly.
- A persistent "Talk to a human" link at the top — discretion is part of the product.
- Dates use a minimal date strip, not a calendar overlay.

## 15. Brand Don'ts (the short list)

- ❌ Globe icons.
- ❌ Airplane silhouettes that aren't ours.
- ❌ Monument collages.
- ❌ Multi-color gradients outside the defined three.
- ❌ Stock smiling families on beaches.
- ❌ Exclamation-mark headlines.
- ❌ "Book now!! ✈️🌴" type captions.
- ❌ Full-page gold backgrounds.
- ❌ Outlined logo.
- ❌ The wordmark on a busy photo without a darkening overlay.

## 16. The Closing Standard

A new piece of work is on-brand if the answer to all five of these is **yes**:

1. Could this exist on the homepage of a luxury airline?
2. Is the gold doing one job and only one job?
3. Could a competitor make this exact piece tomorrow? (If yes — go again.)
4. Does the calm survive when the volume goes up?
5. Would the *Quiet Professional* show this to a friend without explaining it?

If yes — ship it. If no — go back to silence and start again.

Premium Content
System Studio

A productized retainer business for a 2-partner video & branding studio in Tunisia. Six-month roadmap from zero to 10 000+ TND MRR, anchored by a sharp ICP and high-ticket positioning.

Target: 3 000 TND/mo by month 3 · 10 000+ TND/mo by month 6  |  Team: 2 partners (editing + branding + marketing)  |  Market: Tunisian DTC e-commerce + premium service businesses

1 · Strategic foundation

The one assumption everything rests on

This entire system depends on one strategic call: who you sell to, and at what ticket. Get this wrong and the offer, acquisition, and delivery all collapse. Get it right and 3 000 TND/mo is the floor, not the ceiling.

The qualifier rule (apply to every prospect):
Their average customer must be worth ≥ 1 500 TND to them. If yes → a 2 500 TND/mo retainer is a rounding error on their P&L. If no → you're selling a Rolex to someone who needed a watch, and they will churn at month 2.

Why "local Tunisian businesses" mostly fails the qualifier

A neighborhood café, retail shop, or generalist service has a customer LTV of 50–300 TND. They cannot rationally pay a premium retainer — even if they want to, the unit economics break. Only a slice of "local Tunisia" works: high-LTV service businesses.

Refined ICP — two segments, one qualifier

Segment Examples Why they pay premium
Primary
Tunisian DTC e-commerce		 Cosmetics, skincare, modest fashion, streetwear, supplements, home/lifestyle, specialty F&B. Brands doing 25k+ TND/mo, running Meta ads. Secondary

Shared trait: high customer LTV. That's what justifies premium pricing. You're not selling video — you're selling more revenue per ad / per square meter / per chair.

On-location production: a real cost line

Service businesses don't have raw footage to send. Build a half-day shoot template: 4 hours on-site = 30+ short clips of staff, space, procedures, testimonials → fuels one month of content. Either one partner shoots while the other backs up files in real-time, or partner with a videographer at 90–150 TND / half-day. Build it into the price; never quote it separately.

3 · Revenue model

Why low-ticket volume is rejected

15 one-off video packs at 200 TND each = 3 000 TND, but it produces zero recurrence, zero leverage, and burnout in week three. It also contradicts the "system" positioning — discounting destroys credibility. We do not pursue this path.

Three viable paths to 3 000+ TND MRR (high-ticket, fewer clients)

Path Mix MRR When
Floor (M3 target) 2 × Content OS 5 000 TND Month 3
Realistic (M4–5) 1 Performance + 1 Content OS + 1 Ad Pack 10 200 TND Month 4–5
Stretch (M6) 2 Performance + 1 Content OS + 1 Ad Pack 14 700 TND Month 6
Capacity rule: cap at 4 active retainers. Past that, hire a contractor or refuse work. Premium positioning fails the moment delivery quality slips.

The one metric to watch

MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue from retainers). Not followers. Not DMs sent. Not video views. Retainer count × average ticket = the only number that tells you the system is working.

"Working" threshold: MRR grows ≥ 30% month-over-month for the first 4 months. If it doesn't, the bottleneck is acquisition, not delivery.

Bullseye narrowing — pick three channels, double down on whichever produces a paying client first

Cut on day 1: SEO (too slow), Google Ads (no LTV data yet), YouTube (production cost), prospecting low-LTV local businesses.

Free e-com prospecting workflow (2 hours, 60–80 qualified leads)

  1. Open Meta Ad Library → filter Tunisia → keyword by category (cosmétique, mode, supplément, déco…)
  2. Any brand running ads for 30+ days = has budget, has bad creative.
  3. Add to Notion sheet → check IG follower count > 5k → score by ad longevity.
  4. Cold DM with a reference to their actual ad — generic outreach is invisible to them.

Service-business prospecting

  • Google Maps + filter by category (clinique esthétique Tunis, etc.)
  • Cross-reference with Instagram presence (must already post regularly)
  • LinkedIn search for "fondateur / directeur" of those orgs
  • Local agency partnerships — many Tunisian agencies sell SEO/ads but outsource video. Be their video partner, not their competitor.

4 · Acquisition (cont.)

The audit is the real sales asset

Each free audit is a 5-minute Loom + a 1-page mockup:

  • Walk-through of their 5 latest videos / ads
  • 3 specific fixes (color inconsistency, hook structure, pacing)
  • 1 mockup of how their next reel could look in a fixed system

The audit is the moment they realize you think differently. Build a Loom template once; reuse forever.

Communication rules (kill scope creep before it starts)

  • One channel per client (WhatsApp Business OR Slack Connect — pick once, stick).
  • Async-first. Calls only at onboarding + monthly review.
  • Templated Monday status update (3 lines: shipped / in progress / blocked).
  • Revisions: bundled, 1 round per deliverable, 24h client window. Past that, locked.

Three operational moves that compound (the moat)

  1. On-location production becomes core — service businesses can't send footage; build the half-day shoot template once.
  2. UGC brief library for e-com — 10 reusable briefs (unboxing, before/after, problem-solution, testimonial, demo) you hand to the client's UGC creators. You edit; they shoot.
  3. Ad performance loop (Performance & Ad Pack tiers) — read-only access to client's Meta Ads Manager → pull CTR, thumb-stop rate, hook rate weekly → next month's creative briefed from data, not taste. No Tunisian editor does this. It's your moat.

7 · Profit & investment structure

Avoid 50/50. It breaks the moment one partner outworks the other. Use a contribution-weighted model from day one.

9 · What to cut — saying no is half the value

Every "no" below protects the system. Each one is a real temptation that has killed early-stage studios.

The qualifier rule, restated

Before any sales call, write the answer to one question: "What's this prospect's average customer worth to them?" If you can't answer ≥ 1 500 TND with confidence, the call ends as a polite decline. You don't need every client. You need the right four.

Executive summary (one page)

The bet

Position as a "Content System Studio," not a video editor. Build brand+editorial systems first, then produce in them as a recurring retainer. Premium ticket, fewer clients, deeper relationships.

Who we sell to

Tunisian DTC e-commerce (cosmetics, fashion, supplements — running Meta ads) and premium service businesses (clinics, real estate, education). Qualifier: avg customer LTV ≥ 1 500 TND.

What we sell

4 SKUs: Identity Sprint (1 800 one-shot) · Content OS (2 500/mo) · Performance Partner (4 500/mo) · Ad Creative Pack (3 200/mo).

How we get clients

3 channels: IG cold DM (referencing their actual ads/posts), studio IG/TikTok account, LinkedIn for FR-speaking founders. Free 5-min audit Loom = the real sales asset.

The metric

MRR. Threshold: ≥ 30% MoM growth for 4 months. Cap at 4 active retainers — past that, hire or refuse.

The 6-month destination

10 000+ TND MRR from 3–4 retainers, 1 contractor, 1 agency referral partnership. Floor of 3 000 TND hit by month 3.

The five rules that protect the system

  1. The qualifier rule — every prospect's customer must be worth ≥ 1 500 TND to them.
  2. The capacity rule — never more than 4 active retainers per 2 partners.
  3. The vocabulary rule — never say "video editing" or "montage vidéo".
  4. The retainer rule — no transactional gigs once 2 retainers are signed.
  5. The split rule — 40% base / 60% hours-variable from day 1, no exceptions.
